    Junk Science

    • Diets with high-fat cheese, high-fat meat, or carbohydrate on cardiovascular risk markers in overweight postmenopausal women: a randomized crossover trial - 2 week trial, 14 women = not nearly enough to establish cause
    • Meta-analysis of prospective cohort studies evaluating the association of saturated fat with cardiovascular disease - Badly done and misleading. If you group people up in an observational study, some people will have different starting points for their cholesterol because of their genes. If you do an interventional study, you will be able to affect people's cholesterol levels.
    • The A to Z Weight Loss Study: A Randomized Clinical Trial - impact factor 35 (world class). Measured what participants understood of the diet, but didnt follow it fully. Ornish diets followers ate 36% fat instead of 10% and ate a very low fiber diet of 19g a day.
    • Trans fats, but not saturated fats, linked to greater risk of death and heart disease: McMaster study -Embargoed "Using unadjusted data, the study found that people whose diets were heaviest in saturated fat had a 12 percent higher risk of developing heart disease and a 20 percent higher risk of dying of it" source source2
    • Vegetarians Found To Have More Cancer, Allergies And Mental Health Disorders - Impact factor of 3.234(below good). Study itself just gathers data. The site came to an extreme conculsion based on a survey of 300 people.
    • Low-carbohydrate, high-protein diet score and risk of incident cancer - a prospective cohort study Impact factor 3.916 (good). Subjective grouping ruined this study: "The LCHP score is independent of total energy intake, due to the isocaloric nature of carbohydrate and protein, and it allows separate consideration of the amount and quality of fat consumed.". Why not write down macro ratios and keep more metrics for better analysis? Study admits to connection between meat and breast, colon and colorectal cancer. Overall, very biased study. Wording is very much pro-high protein. Also when citing other meat/cancer studies, it ignores large studies that have shown a connection.
    • Serum total cholesterol concentration and 10-year mortality in an 85-year-old population - Very low impact factor of 2.65. Japanese diet of elderly people is almost devoid of processed foods
    • Dietary protein: an essential nutrient for bone health - Impact factor 1.676 (junk). Seems like a biased study. Recommends IGF1 and quotes:"no consistent evidence for superiority of vegetal over animal proteins on calcium metabolism, bone loss prevention and risk reduction of fragility fractures".
    • Insulin-like growth factors and their binding proteins in prostate cancer: cause or consequence? - Impact factor 2.089 (junk). This study suggests that IGF-1 doesnt cause but progesses cancer once its already there. The problem is that we can have millions of different cells with cancer at any time. Our body handles them until they multiply agressively enough. So if IGF-1 encourages them to multiply aggressively, it is basically causing the cancer cells.
